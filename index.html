About Me

I grew up in Bloomsburg, PA to a caring and loving family that provided everything for me in those formable years, but that doesn't mean I didn't have my own unique struggles... I'm the oldest of 4 brothers and have all the cliche markups of an oldest sibling. I thrive on knowledge and learning and taking on new challenges always excites me.

I earned my Bachelor's in Biology from Duquesne University in 2014. Afterwards, I continued my education at Bloomsburg University of Pennsylvania and earned my Master's in Exercise Science in 2016. Under my faculty mentor, I developed an interest in research and completed my thesis of muscle activation patterns with different kinetic forms.

In 2014, my best friend and I started a for-students, by-students ride sharing and food delivery service called ZoomU. After developing the business proposal, we successfully secured $60,000 in funding to develop the idea. Over the next 2 years, we established a Limited Liability Company , worked collaboratively with international developers to build our Minimum Viable Product (MVP), and began onboarding users and independent contractors. in our first year in operation, we served over 3,000 orders to 1,600 users with a team of 10 drivers and developed programs to scale. After the first year, we struggled to get VC funding and retain drivers - because who knew college kids would rather be social than work on weekends?! Through many trials and tribulations, we decided to halt operations in 2017 to pursue other ventures, but the lessons learned have stuck with me.

The transferrable skills and interests landed me my first career-building opportunity as a Program Coordinator for the Institute for Concussion Services at Bloomsburg University. In partnership with the Department of Defense and NCAA, Bloomsburg University screened and monitored over 800 student athletes for cognitive and behavioral traits before and after traumatic brain injuries and established a new clinical program in conjunction with the athletic training department.

As I continued to support the NCAA and it's athletes, I continued my education and earned a Master's in Business Administration from Bloomsburg University in 2020. This experience changed my mission, vision, and values forever. I learned the importance of ledgers and automated systems. In 2018, I developed a new obsession, Blockchain, Cryptocurrency and Web 3.0. Over the next 3 years, I studied the inner workings of blockchain technology and read over 300 white papers to understand the up-and-coming technologies in the space and their respective use cases.

While I continued my studies in blockchain technologies, I moved to Philadelphia for an opportunity in Healthcare. Through 3 years at Main Line Health, I progressed from a Research Assistant studying Population Health and Obstetrics, to a Project Manager studying the effects of cannabinoids on chemotherapy-induced peripheral neuropathy, and eventually to the Business Manager for the Department of OBGYN where I managed day-to-day operations of the administration for 4 hospitals, 120 doctors, 500 nurses, and 8,000 mothers delivering babies annually.

While working in healthcare administration, one of the biggest frustrations was organizational change management and process automation. I realized the shortcoming of the healthcare industries technological developments, which extended from my experience as the Chief Operating Officer of a startup company who paid tens of thousands to develop an application. So I enrolled in a Full Stack Developer Bootcamp with the University of Pennsylvania to pursue my dream of contributing to the continued development of technology, especially blockchain technology.

As the world continues to digitize information and processes, my dream is to contribute to the advancement of technology to create a better future for our kin. My hope is that blockchain technology will transform the economic fabric of our society, giving fair and free opportunity to everyone while maintaining transparent accountability from governments and elites.

It will change our lives forever. It's our responsibility to work with technology to create a better life for everyone.

The blockchain revolution has shown us the power of an immutable ledger, decentralized in control and encrypted for security. The powers of the world can return to the people in a trustless system, where no CEO's, Boards, or Governments can hinder development or take shady backdoor deals.
